• How To Bend A Soccer Ball

    An explination of the kicking technique and physical forces that cause this surreal effect.

  • Alignment

    The first step in bending comes before contact with the soccer ball is made. Instead of pointing your body in the direction of the target, you must approach the ball at a slight angle.

  • The Kick

    When kicking, you should use the inside of your foot to come across the outside of the ball, causing the ball to spin.

  • The "Magnus" Lift Force

    The Magnus effect is a physical phenomenon that occurs when a spinning object that moves through a fluid experiences a force perpendicular to its motion.

  • The "Magnus" Lift Force

    The effect happens because the spin of the ball causes different pressures to appear around it. The difference in pressure around the ball cause an inward net force on it.

    The strength of the Magnus force is proportional to the cross-product of the ball's angular velocity and the ball's forward velocity through the air.

  • Different Bends

    This effect is used in many ways during a game. You can not only make the ball swing left and right, but also make it dip quickly with overspin or make the ball stay aloft longer with underspin.
